Overview

This honors capstone project resulted in a full-functioning prototype of a Chrome extension for online shoppers called SAZANI. This Chrome extension allows shoppers to input their measurements and then see how item sizes compare in order to enhance their satisfaction and decrease return rates. Through user research of females aged 18-25, there are several reasons for the high rates of clothing returns, including inaccurate product descriptions, sizing issues, and differences in color or quality.

Background

As consumers of online shopping, we are frequently faced with challenges. Purchasing clothing items have slowly become a blind activity due to the inaccuracy of sizing and relying on a sizing chart to accurately predict how a piece of clothing will fit different body types. For years, sizing charts have been the norm for how companies and consumers communicate about size. However, returns have become increasingly more common as online shopping consumes our homes. The amount of money lost can impact both the business and the consumers. Why is that the case? Many purchases are being returned due to size inaccuracies even when users utilize the given sizing charts. The goal of SAZANI is to create a space to improve customers’ online shopping user experience where women feel empowered and confident before they purchase the next new item.

Understanding Related Works

Amazon Virtual Try-On

Amazon's virtual try-on feature is an interactive mobile experience that allows customers to visualize how an item will look on themselves. This system uses a front-facing camera to display an image overlay of the item on your body. However, it's important to note that the virtual try-on feature may not always provide an accurate representation of how the clothing item will fit in reality. Additionally, factors such as body shape, proportions, and the way fabric drapes can affect the fit and appearance of clothing. This feature is also currently limited to mainly shoes and does not have anything to do with specific size fitting.

User Research

The user research for the surveys and interviews focused on understanding the shopping behaviors of females ages 18-25 and their needs with online shopping. The 25-question survey was conducted via QuestionPro and yielded 106 participants, with over 30 participants willing to be further interviewed.

Results & Takeaways

The research has shown that online shopping has become increasingly popular in recent years, with a significant portion of consumers purchasing clothing online. However, online shopping has also led to an increase in clothing returns, which can have negative economic, environmental, and social impacts. Given the time span and participant accessibility of this project, a broader audience could have been reached with additional time. The target audience for this research included all females aged 18 to 25, excluding females outside of this range and the entire male population. Through extensive research and peer feedback, it is evident a virtual mannequin chrome extension could be beneficial for both consumers and clothing companies.
