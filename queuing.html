Office-X Queuing System

SMAD Capstone Project

  1. Projects
  2. Office-X Queuing System

Overview

The goal of this system for student users is to give them the ability to join a virtual queue for their professors' office hours and select meeting options that are relevant to their needs. To satisfy this requirement, there is a virtual line that alerts students when they are next in line for office hours. This prevents students from wasting time and clogging the hallways. Students also have the option to select a meeting "reason for visit." This gives professors a sense of the topic that each student needs to discuss and the amount of time the meeting may take. The goal for faculty users is to provide a portal that improves their ability to manage and hold office hours. To achieve this goal, the system gives professors the necessary information (such as typical meeting duration and student participation) to plan and offer office hour sessions. Each faculty member has a virtual queue bar where they can see which students are in line and the reason for meeting. Faculty also have the ability to control meeting duration times.

Background

The office hour system within the College of Business at JMU is chaotic and inefficient for both professors and students. There are notoriously students crowding the hallways and waiting hours for their turn to drop in to office hours with their professor. This poses a safety issue along with extensive time wasted waiting to ask a question. This system's approach is to eliminate the chaos by providing a queue system that allows office hours to run smoothly, time efficiently, and orderly. It will virtually line up students who are waiting to meet with their professors on a drop-in basis.

User Research

Surveys on students' experience during office hours were sent out to several business students. The results yielded similar feedback regarding wants and needs for a more efficient office hour system. Through our results, 2 personas were created. One that highlighted the student perspective, and the one that highlighted the faculty perspective.

Student Design

Student Login

If the student user has already created an account, they will be able to sign into their account with their username and password.

Student Sign-up

If the student user has not already made an account, they will create one by inputting their first name, last name, JMU email address, phone number, and new username.

Student Home

Once a student user has logged in, they are shown their dashboard. Their dashboard allows users to view their profile which stores the information inputted when creating an account and can be changed at any time.

Office Hour Sign-up

From the dashboard, student users have the ability to sign up for office hours and join the queue. Once users are directed to the sign-up page, they can search for faculty by name and select the desired time block. Student users are then able to select the specific class and their meeting purpose from a drop-down.

Student Home

After a student user signs up for a faculty's office hour block, they are directed back to their dashboard where they will be able to click and cancel their spot in the queue if desired. The dashboard also shows student users their queue position and status. When a student is notified by their professor that they are meeting now, the students will receive an email and their status will change from "waiting" to "ready".

Faculty Design

Faculty Login

If the faculty user has already created an account, they will be able to sign into their account with their username and password.

Faculty Sign-up

If the faculty user has not already made an account, they will create one by inputting their first name, last name, JMU email address, phone number, office location, and new username.

Faculty Class Selection

Once the faculty is in the system they will select the classes they wish to open office hours to. This will give students the option to specify which class they are visiting this professor for.

Office Hour Manager

Faculty members have the ability to create office hour blocks including the time and location. These blocks could also be recurring if the professor would like to have weekly meetings at the same time on the same day.

Faculty Home

The faculty home page allows users to see scheduled office hour blocks and navigate to the queue and office hour manager.

Queue Manager

Once an office hour block is in session the faculty can see students in the queue. From this page faculty users can notify a student when it is their turn to arrive for office hours. If the student does not show up, the professor can mark the student as a no-show.

Results & Takeaways

This collaborative capstone has allowed me to experience being part of a design team. In creating designs for this queuing system I had to take on creative freedoms while adhering to the system's development and compromising with 10 other teammates. To ensure a smooth running system the user interface code had to be troubleshooted when transferred to the final system. This client work was valuable as a final major project for senior year, allowing me to gain design experience apart from my professors.
