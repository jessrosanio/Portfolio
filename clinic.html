Blue Ridge Free Clinic

Advertising Deliverables

  1. Projects
  2. Blue Ridge Free Clinic Deliverables

Overview

As Media Arts and Design students, we were each asked to produce our own deliverables that would benefit Blue Ridge Free Clinic's advertising. The deliverables consisted of a trifold brochure, 3 Facebook posts, and an alternative piece of merchandise such as a sticker. Each piece of advertising material stayed consistent with the clinic's already existing color scheme and information displayed on its website.

    Roles:

  • Graphic Designer

    Timeline:

  • Aug 2021 - Dec 2021

Background

As part of my SMAD 201 - Foundations of Visual Communication Design class, we were asked to partner with the Blue Ridge Free Clinic to create a variety of deliverables to update their marketing. As a non-profit clinic new to Harrisonburg, they do not have much material to advertise their business to the community as most of their members are hands-on in the clinic.

Deliverables

Brochures

For all of these deliverables, I decided to use a darker blue and some orange font to show the colors of Blue Ridge Free Clinic. In the brochure important information is highlighted such as the story, the clinic’s services, staff, appointment info, and contact info. This will allow patients, donors, and community members to get a general idea of what The Blue Ridge Free Clinic does and offers for their patients. I made sure to include headshots of staff members to introduce their titles, and a fun picture below the story to show the bonding of the development of this clinic.

Facebook Posts

The social media designs are very simple with some text and rounded rectangles containing important information. The profile picture is Blue Ridge Free Clinic’s logo and every caption contains the slogan “Your Bridge to Health”. The first Facebook post consists of what patients should bring to their appointments. It is a simple overview and patients can go to the website given in the comment for more information. The second Facebook post encourages donors to donate to support the clinic. I have displayed the QR code to bring someone straight to the Venmo page and below is the username for anyone who would like to look up and find their page manually. Lastly, the third Facebook post shows the days the clinic is open for patients to visit. This is very simple but important information for community members to know.

Sticker

For the alternative media, I decided to make a sticker for volunteers to put on anything like a laptop or water bottle. This is a good way to spread information by word around the community. Any college student would gladly take a sticker and when asked about it then can briefly explain the purpose of the clinic to others which can help raise awareness.

Results & Takeaways

Through this process, I learned to understand the importance of consistency in design. Between the colors, fonts, shapes, etc. it was vital that I kept every deliverable looking related to each other. This was also my first early on client experience where I learned to work with what I was given. Many pictures and graphics had to work given varying dimensions and quality.
